### Byword: Strategy and Why It Works

**Dre:**  
Tell us about Byword.

**Jake:**  
Byword is an AI content platform focused on SEO at scale. We launched right before ChatGPT took off. Before the SaaS product, we were doing consulting and had a client case that reached around 1M monthly organic traffic using AI content workflows.

**Dre:**  
What’s the strategy behind it?

**Jake:**  
AI’s biggest advantage is scale.  
If you wanted 300 pages traditionally, it could cost tens of thousands and take months. With AI, you can generate and deploy much faster and cheaper.

A practical workflow:
1. Start with seed keywords.  
2. Use tools like Ahrefs/Semrush to find matching terms.  
3. Build keyword frameworks (e.g., “industry blog name ideas”).  
4. Expand variables (industries, modifiers, etc.).  
5. Generate content in bulk from that dataset.

**Dre:**  
What makes Byword different?

**Jake:**  
A few things:
- Bulk generation at scale  
- Custom templates for programmatic SEO page types  
- Auto internal linking logic based on sitemap/context  
- CMS publishing workflows (draft/scheduled/publish)  
- Integrations and optimization workflows

**Dre:**  
How do you handle AI quality and “humanizing” content?

**Jake:**  
Most people skip testing. Before publishing hundreds of pages, test output on a handful of topics first. Iterate prompts/templates/tone/sections until quality is right.  
That way, most quality work is solved *before* publishing instead of editing hundreds of pages after.

### Hook Writing Advice

**Dre:**  
You’re great at hooks. How do you write them?

**Jake:**  
I keep a swipe file of strong hooks across platforms (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Reddit, video titles, etc.).  
Then:
1. Adapt proven structures
2. Make it specific to your own real experience/data
3. Write multiple hook variants (I do ~5)
4. Get quick feedback from others before posting

Emotion, contrast, specificity, and numbers often help — but don’t make it boring.
